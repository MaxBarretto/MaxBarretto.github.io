   1/10: What Makes a Brand?

To be successful, your brand needs to have a wide appeal and a recognizable logo. A complex or simple logo can work, but having a simple design that is easy to draw can really catch the eye. The main thing that you need is a consistant color scheme that people can associate with your brand. For example, IKEA is known for the yellow and blue pallette that is in their logo and both store interior and exterior. To make a new brand, you want a simple design that communicates the image and style of the brand, so stylized acronyms are a great place to start.

   1/27: Women in E-Sports

At least at a college level, E-Sports are by all means a male majority sport, and seeing women in competition is rare due to some severe obstacles that need to be tackled in the coming years. Due to factors like harassment, toxic masculinity, and stereotyping, girls are discouraged to play games that they might otherwise win at. No matter who you are, you should be able to have a chance to win at any competition, and once the E-Sports community is more diverse, it will encourage other people to play, growing the overall fanbase and competition.

   2/6: E-Sports Communication

E-Sports revolve around good teamwork, and some major aspects of that involve being a good listener, making your intentions clear, have patience, and be flexible to any changes on the fly. Without these skills, even the strongest teams will fail, making individual skills pointless. Some strategies that my teams can incorporate are shieldbreaks in Smash Bros, and sandbagging in Mario Kart to get better items. To improve our communication, we can play some fun friendly games, for example, having two people play blindfolded while the other two instruct how to play. Upon actually trying this excercise, it proved to be exceedingly difficult, but it was also a blast and it helped communicate directions at least a little better.

   2/8: E-Sports Toxicity

The gaming space has been polluted with overwhelming toxicity in recent years, largely due to the accessibility of microphones with a lack of necessary punishments for saying insults online. When someone starts spreading hate, the ramifications of physical and emotional distress can hurt performance even at the highest level of competition, so the responsibility is on the part of game companies to intervene and block accounts that promote the kind of behaviour that shouldn't be tolerated. Systems like the "Tribunal" system by the League of Legends publishers Riot Games allowed people to vote on whether certain instances of bad behaviour were tolerable, which is a step in the right direction, although the service ended shortly after release.
